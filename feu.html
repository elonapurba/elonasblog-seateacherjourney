Academic Support System

FEU Roosevelt College in Cainta, Philippines, offers a robust academic support system designed to enhance student learning and development across various educational levels. Here are the key components of this system:

1. Comprehensive Academic Programs

  • Basic Education: Preschool to Senior High School, with strands such as:
    • ABM (Accountancy, Business, and Management)
    • STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)
    • HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)
  • Undergraduate Programs: Degrees in Education, Business Administration, Hospitality Management, and Information Technology.
  • Graduate Programs: Advanced degrees in Education and other fields, promoting professional growth and specialization.

2. Learning Resources and Facilities

  • Libraries: Access to extensive collections of books, journals, and online resources.
  • Technology Integration: Classrooms feature advanced technology including computers and multimedia projectors to facilitate interactive learning experiences.

3. Innovative Teaching Methods

  • Blended Learning Approaches: Combining traditional classroom instruction with online resources to cater to diverse learning preferences.
  • Dynamic Curriculum: A flexible curriculum that adapts to the changing educational landscape and student needs.

4. Faculty Development

  • Training Programs: Regular workshops and training sessions help faculty stay updated on the latest teaching methodologies and technologies.

5. Student Support Services

  • Academic Advising: Guidance from faculty advisors helps students navigate their academic paths effectively.
  • Counselling Services: Support for students' mental health and well-being is available through counselling services.

6. Extracurricular Activities

  • Clubs and Organizations: Various student organizations foster leadership skills, teamwork, and social engagement.
  • Events and Competitions: Regular events provide opportunities for students to showcase their talents and skills beyond academics.

This comprehensive academic support system at FEU Roosevelt College aims to cultivate well-rounded individuals who are prepared for the challenges of the modern workforce while fostering a culture of excellence and lifelong learning.

Teaching System

FEU Roosevelt College Teaching System

FEU Roosevelt College in Cainta employs a modern and dynamic teaching system that integrates various educational strategies and technologies to enhance student learning.

Key Components

    Curriculum Framework

    • K-12 Curriculum: The college adheres to the K-12 educational framework, which emphasizes a comprehensive approach to education, fostering competencies that prepare students for both higher education and employment.
    • Dynamic Curriculum: The curriculum is designed to be flexible and responsive to the needs of students, promoting collaboration among teachers and students.

    Teaching Methodologies

    • Blended Learning: FEU Roosevelt utilizes blended learning strategies that combine traditional face-to-face instruction with online resources, allowing for a more engaging learning experience.
    • Collaborative Learning: The teaching system encourages teamwork and collaboration through group activities, discussions, and projects, fostering critical thinking and communication skills.

    Use of Technology

    • Learning Management System (LMS): The college employs Canvas as its LMS, enabling students to access course materials, submit assignments, and participate in online assessments.
    • Remote Learning Capabilities: With platforms like Microsoft Teams, FEU Roosevelt facilitates remote learning sessions, ensuring continuity of education regardless of location.

    Assessment and Evaluation

    • Structured Assessment Plans: Teachers develop detailed lesson plans that include clear objectives, subject matter, teaching-learning activities, and assessment methods. This structured approach ensures that learning outcomes are effectively measured.
    • Continuous Feedback: Regular assessments provide students with feedback on their progress, helping them identify areas for improvement.
