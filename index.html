FEU ROOSEVELT COLLEGE CAINTA, PHILIPPHINES

Talk about FEU Roosevelt College Inc. (RCI) it feels less if you do not know about it. Here I will share a little knowledge about FEU Roosevelt College Inc. (RCI). FEU Roosevelt, formerly known as Roosevelt College, is a private, non-sectarian educational institution located in the northeastern area of Metro Manila, Philippines. Established in 1933 as Marikina Academy by former Mayor Wenceslao Dela Paz, it is one of the oldest academic institutions in the region. After closing in 1944 following Dela Paz's death, the school was reestablished as Roosevelt Memorial High School (RMHS) to address the lack of secondary education in Marikina during the postwar period. Over time, RMHS expanded to several locations and began offering tertiary programs, leading to its rebranding as Roosevelt College, Inc. (RCI).

In 2016, RCI became part of the Far Eastern University (FEU) Group of Schools and was rebranded as FEU Roosevelt in 2019. The institution offers a comprehensive range of educational programs, including basic education and tertiary courses across its three campuses: Cainta, Marikina, and Rodriguez. The Cainta campus serves as the flagship location and houses programs from preschool through senior high school. The current president is Juan Miguel R. Montinola, with Alma Emerita Dela Cruz serving as Senior Vice President. The school’s colours are green and gold, and its mascot is Tam Tam, representing the Tamaraws, the school's nickname. The FEU Hymn by Nick Joaquin serves as the institution's anthem. With a commitment to academic excellence and cultural enrichment, FEU Roosevelt continues to play a significant role in education within the region.

Cainta

FEU Roosevelt Cainta was set up in 1974, when then Roosevelt Memorial High School was transferred from San Roque in Marikina to the sprawling Sumulong Highway campus. Aside from Basic Education programs from preschool to senior high school, the Cainta campus also houses FEU Roosevelt’s Institute for Teacher Education.
